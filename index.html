ABOUT WILDLIFE

Wildlife refers to the animals and plants species that live in their natural habitats, outside of human settlements and interference. These species exist in various ecosystems,such as forests, grasslands, deserts, oceans, rivers, and wetlands. They all play important roles in maintaining the balance of the ecosystem, including pollination, seed dispersal, and keeping the food chain in check. Examples ofincluding forests, oceans, mountains, grasslands, and deserts. Wildlife plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of nature and ensuring the survival of different ecosystems. Animals provide us with food, clothing, and other materials, and also contribute to the overall wellbeing of the planet. For example, bees pollinate plants, which helps in producing fruits and vegetables. Similarly, predators help keep the populations of other animals in check, preventing overgrazing and overpopulation. However, human activities such as deforestation, pollution, and hunting have threatened the survival of many wildlife species.
